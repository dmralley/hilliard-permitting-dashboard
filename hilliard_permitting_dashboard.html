Housing Market & Affordability data source: This section is pulled from the City's separate Hilliard Housing Affordability Tracker (gabe-lade.github.io/swank-program/data/hilliard-housing-tracker.html), snapshotted as of 2026 Q1. Sale price, $/sq ft, and home-type figures come from Franklin County Auditor parcel and conveyance records aggregated quarterly by city staff; the "Income Needed" series is a modeled affordability index (10% down, prevailing 30-year mortgage rate, 1.8% effective property tax, $1,200/yr insurance, 0.4% PMI, 30% housing-cost-to-income standard) compared against the HUD Area Median Income for the Columbus metro; rent figures are the Zillow Observed Rent Index; the ACS context charts (household income, renter share, cost burden, housing stock mix) come from American Community Survey 5-year estimates and update annually. Per request, this dashboard omits three panels from the source tracker — the "Hilliard vs Franklin County Median" affordability-ratio KPI/chart, the Zillow typical home value (ZHVI) chart, and the "New Housing Units Permitted" chart (a placeholder in the source; this dashboard already shows real permit data above) — since they either duplicate other panels here or aren't yet backed by real data at the source. To refresh this section, ask to update the Housing Market & Affordability data from the Hilliard Housing Tracker — the source URL above is re-read and the embedded chart data re-extracted each time.

Methodology note: Department permit/revenue totals are pulled directly from OpenGov's built-in Analytics view, filtered by department (Building Standards Division, Engineering Department, Planning Division). These totals include every record type under each department, so they are broader than — and will not exactly match — the narrower "Total Permits / Total Revenue" figures in the separate Monthly Building Standards Division Report workbook (which draws from a specific subset of exported reports). Engineering Department monthly revenue figures do reconcile closely with that workbook (e.g. April $179,635.14, May $273,932.82). "New 2 & 3 Family" and apartment-unit data only go back to early 2024 in OpenGov; no earlier history was available there. The 2012–2023 bars in the combined housing chart come from the City of Hilliard's public ClearGov Transparency Center ("Single Family and Multi-Family Units Permitted," cleargov.com/ohio/franklin/city/hilliard/dashboards/1122/building), which does not split multi-family permits into 2/3-family vs. larger apartment complexes for those years — hence the single "Multi-Family / Apartments" bucket pre-2024. 2024, 2025, and 2026 YTD bars use the OpenGov figures pulled directly above, which do split that category. Code Enforcement case counts reflect "Records Created" in OpenGov's Property Maintenance Complaints category, split into Grass/Weed Complaint vs. all other City of Hilliard Code Violation cases. The "2026 New Home Permits by Subdivision" chart totals all New Single Family and New 2 & 3 Family building permits submitted Jan 1 – Jul 22, 2026, grouped by the Subdivision field on each permit. Permits where OpenGov's Subdivision field was left as "Other" (29 permits, all addressed on Addison Ln N/S) have been reclassified as Leap Rd Townhomes, the actual subdivision at that location; the 3 Tarlton West permits get their own bar; the remaining "Other" bucket is just the single one-off Eagle Pointe permit.
